# Service Level Agreement (SLA) & Operational Level Agreement (OLA)
### Richmond Solutions Inc. — IT Service Management

Document Version: 1.1
Last Updated: 2026
Owner: IT Manager
Practice Area: Service Level Management, Relationship Management

---

### 1. Purpose

This document defines the service expectations, operational responsibilities, and performance targets for IT support services delivered by Richmond Solutions Inc.

The agreement establishes:
- Expected service response times
- Incident resolution targets
- Service availability expectations
- Escalation procedures
- Operational responsibilities between IT teams

This document is divided into:
- Part A — Service Level Agreement (SLA)
- Part B — Operational Level Agreement (OLA)

---
  
### 2. Scope

This agreement applies to:
- All Richmond Solutions employees
- Internally managed IT services
- Service desk operations
- Networking and infrastructure support
- Microsoft 365 services
- Endpoint support

---

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|:-----|:-----------|
| **SLA** | Service Level Agreement — between IT (provider) and business users (customer) |
| **OLA** | Operational Level Agreement — between internal IT teams |
| **UC** | Underpinning Contract — with external suppliers (e.g. ISP, cloud provider) |
| **SLO** | Service Level Objective — a specific measurable target within the SLA |
| **Availability** | Percentage of time a service remains operational |
| **Response Time** | Time between ticket creation and first technician engagement |
| **Resolution Time** | Time required to restore service |

### 4.2. Incident Response & Resolution Targets

Incident Response & Resolution Targets

Priority is assigned based on:
- Business impact
- Number of affected users
- Urgency
- Workaround availability

| Priority | Description | Response Time | Resolution Target |
|:---------|:------------|:--------------|:------------------|
| P1 — Critical | Full service outage, >50 users affected | 15 minutes | 4 hours |
| P2 — High | Major function down, department-wide | 30 minutes | 8 hours |
| P3 — Medium | Degraded service, multiple-user workaround available | 2 hours | 24 hours |
| P4 — Low | Minor issue, single user, workaround available | 4 hours | 72 hours |

**Response time** = time from ticket creation to first analyst contact with user.  

**Resolution target** = target time to restore service (not root cause fix).

Root cause remediation may continue separately under Problem Management.

### Hierarchical Escalation

Escalation to management occurs when:
- SLA breach risk exists
- Business-critical systems are impacted
- Executive users are affected
- Major incidents are declared

If SLA targets are not being met:

1. User raises concern with service desk
2. Service desk escalates to IT Manager within 1 business day
3. IT Manager reviews, responds within 24 hours with resolution plan
4. If unresolved after 5 business days → formal review meeting with department head

- critical business operations are unavailable
- more than 50 users are affected
- organization-wide outage occurs

|:----------|:-------|
| Initial IT Manager notification	| Within 15 minutes |

| Incident bridge activation	| Within 20 minutes |
| Post-Incident Review (PIR)	| Within 48 hours |

### 4.8. Exclusions

The following are outside the scope of this SLA:

- Software not on the approved software list

- Third-party service outages beyond IT's control (e.g. Microsoft 365 outage)

##

### 4.12. Watermelon SLA Prevention

A “Watermelon SLA” occurs when:
- Operational metrics appear healthy (“green”)
but:
- User satisfaction remains poor (“red”)

To avoid this:
- SLA metrics are reviewed alongside satisfaction surveys
- Recurring complaints trigger service review
- Operational KPIs are adjusted if misaligned with user experience

## PART B: OPERATIONAL LEVEL AGREEMENT (OLA)

**Provider:** Richmond Solutions Networking Team (internal)  
**Customer:** IT Service Desk Team  
**Effective Date:** January 2026  
**Purpose:** Define what the networking team commits to deliver in support of the SLA above  
**Review Cycle:** Quarterly

##

### 5.1. Scope

This OLA covers the networking team's responsibilities in supporting incident resolution 
and service delivery for network-related issues at Richmond Solutions.

##

### 5.2. Networking Team Commitments

| Obligation | Target |
|:-----------|:-------|
| Respond to P1 network incidents escalated by service desk | Within 15 minutes during business hours |
| Restore core network connectivity (P1) | Within 2 hours |
| Respond to P2 network incidents | Within 30 minutes |
| Provide network access for new user accounts | Within 1 business day of request |
| Configure VPN access | Within 2 business days of approved request |
| Notify service desk of planned network maintenance | Minimum 48 hours advance notice |
| Provide post-incident network diagnostics | Within 24 hours of P1/P2 resolution |

##

### 5.3. Service Desk Commitments

| Obligation | Target |
|:-----------|:-------|
| Provide complete incident details when escalating to networking | At time of escalation |
| Notify networking of approved change requests affecting network | Minimum 24 hours in advance |
| Update tickets with networking resolution notes | Within 1 hour of resolution |

##

### 5.4. Escalation

If OLA targets are not met, the service desk escalates to the IT Manager who will 
coordinate resolution between teams.

##

### Watermelon SLA — Anti-Pattern Note

A **Watermelon SLA** occurs when metrics appear green (targets met) but the customer is actually unhappy. To prevent this at Northgate Solutions, SLA performance is reviewed alongside quarterly user satisfaction surveys. If survey scores are low despite targets being met, the metrics themselves are reviewed for relevance.
