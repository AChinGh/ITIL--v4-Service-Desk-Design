# Service Level Agreement (SLA) & Operational Level Agreement (OLA)
### Richmond Solutions Inc. — IT Service Management

Document Version: 1.1
Last Updated: 2026
Owner: IT Manager
Practice Area: Service Level Management, Relationship Management

---

### 1. Purpose

This document defines the service expectations, operational responsibilities, and performance targets for IT support services delivered by Richmond Solutions Inc.

The agreement establishes:
- Expected service response times
- Incident resolution targets
- Service availability expectations
- Escalation procedures
- Operational responsibilities between IT teams

This document is divided into:
- Part A — Service Level Agreement (SLA)
- Part B — Operational Level Agreement (OLA)

---
  
### 2. Scope

This agreement applies to:
- All Richmond Solutions employees
- Internally managed IT services
- Service desk operations
- Networking and infrastructure support
- Microsoft 365 services
- Endpoint support

---

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|:-----|:-----------|
| **SLA** | Service Level Agreement — between IT (provider) and business users (customer) |
| **OLA** | Operational Level Agreement — between internal IT teams |
| **UC** | Underpinning Contract — with external suppliers (e.g. ISP, cloud provider) |
| **SLO** | Service Level Objective — a specific measurable target within the SLA |
| **Availability** | Percentage of time a service remains operational |
| **Response Time** | Time between ticket creation and first technician engagement |
| **Resolution Time** | Time required to restore service |
